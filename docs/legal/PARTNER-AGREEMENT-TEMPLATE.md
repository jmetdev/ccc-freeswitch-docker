> jurisdiction review and adapt it before signing or presenting it to any partner.

# CHANNEL PARTNER AGREEMENT

This Channel Partner Agreement (the "**Agreement**") is entered into as of
`[EFFECTIVE DATE]` (the "**Effective Date**") by and between:

- **`[COMPANY LEGAL NAME]`**, a `[STATE/COUNTRY]` `[ENTITY TYPE, e.g., corporation / LLC]`
  with its principal place of business at `[COMPANY ADDRESS]` ("**Company**"); and
- **`[PARTNER LEGAL NAME]`**, a `[STATE/COUNTRY]` `[ENTITY TYPE]` with its principal
  place of business at `[PARTNER ADDRESS]` ("**Partner**").

Company and Partner are each a "**Party**" and together the "**Parties**."

## 1. Definitions

1.1 "**Service**" means Company's hosted software-as-a-service offering known as
`[PRODUCT NAME]`, including any related documentation, updates, and support
services made generally available by Company, but excluding any third-party
products.

1.2 "**Customer**" means an end customer that purchases a subscription to the
Service through Partner under this Agreement.

1.3 "**Customer Agreement**" means Company's then-current end-customer terms of
service, available at `[URL TO TERMS OF SERVICE]`, as updated by Company from
time to time.

1.4 "**Territory**" means `[GEOGRAPHIC TERRITORY / "worldwide" / NAMED MARKET SEGMENT]`.

1.5 "**Order**" means an ordering document or online order submitted by Partner
and accepted by Company for Customer subscriptions to the Service.

1.6 "**Partner Price List**" means Company's then-current partner pricing and
discount schedule, attached as **Exhibit A** or otherwise provided to Partner in
writing.

## 2. Appointment; Relationship

2.1 **Appointment.** Company appoints Partner as a **non-exclusive** reseller of
the Service in the Territory, and Partner accepts the appointment. Partner may
market, promote, and resell subscriptions to the Service to Customers in the
Territory during the Term, subject to this Agreement.

2.2 **No Exclusivity.** Nothing in this Agreement limits Company's right to
market or sell the Service directly or through other partners, inside or outside
the Territory. `[IF GRANTING EXCLUSIVITY, REPLACE THIS SECTION AND ADD MINIMUM
SALES COMMITMENTS.]`

2.3 **Independent Contractors.** The Parties are independent contractors. This
Agreement does not create a partnership, joint venture, franchise, agency, or
employment relationship. Neither Party may bind the other or make commitments on
the other's behalf. Partner will not make any representation, warranty, or
commitment regarding the Service beyond those in Company's published
documentation and the Customer Agreement.

2.4 **Subcontracting; Sub-resellers.** Partner may not appoint sub-resellers or
subcontract its obligations without Company's prior written consent.

## 3. Orders; Provisioning

3.1 **Orders.** Partner will submit Orders in the form and via the process
Company designates. Each Order must identify the Customer, subscription tier,
quantities, subscription term, and fees. Orders are binding when accepted by
Company in writing (email sufficient) or by provisioning the Service. Company
may reject any Order in its reasonable discretion (including for credit,
compliance, or capacity reasons).

3.2 **Customer Agreement Required.** Partner will ensure each Customer accepts
the Customer Agreement before or upon first access to the Service. Partner has
no authority to modify the Customer Agreement or grant rights in the Service
beyond it. Each Customer's use of the Service is governed solely by the Customer
Agreement between Company and the Customer.

3.3 **Provisioning and Support Roles.** Company will provision the Service for
accepted Orders. Support responsibilities are allocated as follows:
`[CHOOSE ONE:]`
- `[OPTION A — Partner provides Tier 1 support; Company provides Tier 2/3
  support to Partner (not directly to Customers).]`
- `[OPTION B — Company provides all support directly to Customers.]`

3.4 **Renewals.** `[STATE WHETHER SUBSCRIPTIONS AUTO-RENEW, WHO OWNS THE RENEWAL,
AND WHETHER PARTNER RETAINS ITS DISCOUNT/MARGIN ON RENEWALS.]`

## 4. Fees; Payment

4.1 **Partner Pricing.** Partner will purchase subscriptions at the prices in
the Partner Price List (Exhibit A). Company may update the Partner Price List on
`[30/60/90]` days' written notice; updates apply to Orders accepted after the
notice period and do not affect in-flight subscription terms.

4.2 **Resale Pricing.** Partner independently sets its resale prices to
Customers, except where a resale price restriction would be unlawful. `[SOME
JURISDICTIONS RESTRICT RESALE PRICE MAINTENANCE — CONFIRM WITH COUNSEL. AN MSRP
MAY BE STATED AS NON-BINDING GUIDANCE.]`

4.3 **Invoicing and Payment.** Company will invoice Partner `[MONTHLY / UPON
ORDER ACCEPTANCE / ANNUALLY IN ADVANCE]`. Invoices are due within `[30]` days of
the invoice date. Late amounts accrue interest at the lesser of `[1.5]`% per
month or the maximum rate permitted by law. **Partner's payment obligations are
not contingent on Partner collecting from Customers.**

4.4 **Taxes.** Fees are exclusive of taxes. Partner is responsible for all
sales, use, VAT, GST, withholding, and similar taxes arising from its resale
activity, excluding taxes on Company's net income. Partner will provide valid
resale/exemption certificates where applicable.

4.5 **Suspension.** Company may suspend provisioning of new Orders, and after
`[15]` days' notice suspend affected subscriptions, if Partner's undisputed
payments are more than `[30]` days past due.

## 5. Partner Obligations

Partner will:

(a) market and resell the Service only in the Territory and in accordance with
this Agreement, applicable law, and Company's published partner policies;

(b) make no representations or warranties about the Service beyond Company's
documentation and marketing materials;

(c) conduct business in a manner that reflects favorably on the Service and
Company, and not engage in deceptive, misleading, or unethical practices;

(d) comply with all applicable laws, including anti-bribery/anti-corruption laws
(e.g., the U.S. FCPA and U.K. Bribery Act), export control and sanctions laws,
and telecommunications regulations applicable to Partner's resale activity
`[RELEVANT FOR VOICE/SIP/CPaaS OFFERINGS — e.g., FCC/robocall rules (STIR/SHAKEN),
E911/emergency-calling obligations, number-portability and lawful-intercept rules
in the Territory]`;

(e) maintain records of its resale activity under this Agreement for `[3]` years
and permit Company, on reasonable notice and no more than once per year, to
audit those records solely to verify compliance;

(f) promptly report to Company any suspected security incident, misuse of the
Service, or infringement of Company's intellectual property of which it becomes
aware; and

(g) meet any minimum performance requirements set out in **Exhibit B** (if any).

## 6. Company Obligations

Company will:

(a) provide the Service to Customers materially in accordance with the Customer
Agreement and Company's published service level commitments at `[SLA URL]`, if any;

(b) provide Partner with reasonable sales enablement, including product
documentation and `[TRAINING / DEMO ENVIRONMENTS / NFR (NOT-FOR-RESALE)
LICENSES]`; and

(c) provide the support allocated to Company under Section 3.3.

## 7. Trademarks; Marketing

7.1 **License to Partner.** Company grants Partner a non-exclusive,
non-transferable, revocable license during the Term to use Company's name, logo,
and product marks solely to market and resell the Service in the Territory, in
accordance with Company's brand guidelines. All goodwill inures to Company.

7.2 **License to Company.** Partner grants Company the right to identify Partner
as an authorized partner (name and logo) on Company's website and marketing
materials during the Term.

7.3 **Restrictions.** Neither Party will register or use domain names, social
media handles, or trademarks confusingly similar to the other Party's marks.
Partner will cease all use of Company marks upon termination.

## 8. Intellectual Property

8.1 Company and its licensors retain all right, title, and interest in and to
the Service, documentation, and all related intellectual property. No rights are
granted to Partner except as expressly stated in this Agreement. Partner
acquires no ownership interest by reselling the Service.

8.2 Partner will not (and will not permit anyone to): reverse engineer,
decompile, or disassemble the Service except as permitted by law; copy, modify,
or create derivative works of the Service; access the Service to build a
competing product; or resell outside the Territory or bundle the Service in a
manner not authorized in writing by Company.

8.3 **Feedback.** Company may freely use feedback or suggestions Partner
provides, without obligation.

## 9. Confidentiality

9.1 "**Confidential Information**" means non-public information disclosed by one
Party to the other that is designated confidential or that reasonably should be
understood to be confidential, including the Partner Price List, product
roadmaps, and Customer data. It excludes information that is or becomes public
without breach, was rightfully known before disclosure, is independently
developed, or is rightfully received from a third party.

9.2 The receiving Party will use Confidential Information only to perform under
this Agreement, protect it with at least reasonable care, and not disclose it
except to employees, advisors, and contractors bound by obligations at least as
protective. Disclosures compelled by law are permitted with prompt notice where
lawful. These obligations survive for `[3/5]` years after termination (and
indefinitely for trade secrets).

## 10. Data Protection

10.1 As between the Parties, Company processes Customer personal data as
described in the Customer Agreement and Company's data processing addendum at
`[DPA URL]`.

10.2 Partner will comply with applicable data protection laws (including, where
applicable, GDPR, UK GDPR, and CCPA/CPRA) in its marketing and sales activities,
will only share personal data with Company on a lawful basis, and will promptly
notify Company of any data subject request or regulator inquiry relating to the
Service that it receives.

## 11. Warranties; Disclaimer

11.1 **Mutual.** Each Party represents that it is duly organized, has authority
to enter into this Agreement, and will comply with applicable law in its
performance.

11.2 **Service Warranty to Customers.** Any warranty for the Service is provided
by Company to Customers solely under the Customer Agreement. **Partner is not
authorized to make warranties on Company's behalf.**

11.3 **Disclaimer.** EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY STATED IN THIS AGREEMENT, THE SERVICE
AND ALL COMPANY MATERIALS ARE PROVIDED "AS IS," AND COMPANY DISCLAIMS ALL OTHER
WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A
PARTICULAR PURPOSE, TITLE, AND NON-INFRINGEMENT, TO THE MAXIMUM EXTENT PERMITTED
BY LAW.

## 12. Indemnification

12.1 **By Company.** Company will defend Partner against third-party claims
alleging that the Service, as provided by Company and used as authorized,
infringes a third party's intellectual property rights, and will pay damages
finally awarded or settlement amounts approved by Company. Company may, at its
option, procure rights, modify the Service, or terminate affected subscriptions
with a pro-rata refund. This Section states Company's entire liability for
infringement claims.

12.2 **By Partner.** Partner will defend Company against third-party claims
arising from: (a) Partner's representations or warranties beyond those
authorized; (b) Partner's violation of law (including telecom, export, anti-
corruption, and data protection laws); or (c) Partner's agreements or dealings
with Customers or prospects, except to the extent caused by Company's breach of
this Agreement; and will pay damages finally awarded or approved settlements.

12.3 **Procedure.** The indemnified Party must give prompt notice, sole control
of defense and settlement to the indemnifying Party (no settlement admitting
fault by, or imposing obligations on, the indemnified Party without its
consent), and reasonable cooperation.

## 13. Limitation of Liability

13.1 EXCLUDING LIABILITY UNDER SECTION 12 (INDEMNIFICATION), BREACH OF SECTION 9
(CONFIDENTIALITY), PARTNER'S PAYMENT OBLIGATIONS, OR A PARTY'S WILLFUL
MISCONDUCT: (a) NEITHER PARTY WILL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL,
CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR LOST PROFITS, REVENUE, OR DATA; AND (b)
EACH PARTY'S TOTAL LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT WILL NOT EXCEED THE AMOUNTS
PAID OR PAYABLE BY PARTNER TO COMPANY IN THE `[12]` MONTHS BEFORE THE EVENT
GIVING RISE TO LIABILITY. THESE LIMITS APPLY TO THE MAXIMUM EXTENT PERMITTED BY
LAW AND REGARDLESS OF THE THEORY OF LIABILITY.

## 14. Term; Termination

14.1 **Term.** This Agreement starts on the Effective Date and continues for
`[1/2]` year(s), automatically renewing for successive `[1]`-year terms unless
either Party gives non-renewal notice at least `[60]` days before the end of the
then-current term (together, the "**Term**").

14.2 **Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate this Agreement
for convenience on `[60/90]` days' written notice. `[OPTIONAL — DELETE IF NOT
DESIRED.]`

14.3 **Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate: (a) on `[30]` days'
written notice of a material breach that remains uncured; or (b) immediately if
the other Party becomes insolvent, makes an assignment for the benefit of
creditors, or is subject to bankruptcy proceedings not dismissed within `[60]`
days. Company may terminate immediately on written notice for Partner's breach
of Sections 5(d) (compliance with law), 7 (trademarks), or 8 (IP).

14.4 **Effect of Termination.** Upon termination or expiry: Partner will stop
marketing the Service and using Company marks; unpaid fees for accepted Orders
become due; and each Party will return or destroy the other's Confidential
Information on request. **Existing Customer subscriptions continue for their
current terms** under the Customer Agreement; `[CHOOSE: Company will honor
Partner's margin on those subscriptions through the end of their current terms /
Company may transition Customers to direct billing or another partner]`.

14.5 **Survival.** Sections 1, 4 (for accrued amounts), 8, 9, 10, 11.3, 12, 13,
14.4, 14.5, and 15 survive termination.

## 15. General

15.1 **Governing Law; Venue.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of
`[STATE/COUNTRY]`, excluding conflict-of-laws rules and the U.N. Convention on
Contracts for the International Sale of Goods. The Parties consent to the
exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the courts located in `[VENUE]`.
`[ALTERNATIVE: BINDING ARBITRATION CLAUSE.]`

15.2 **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other's
prior written consent, except to a successor in a merger, acquisition, or sale
of substantially all assets, with notice. Any other assignment is void.

15.3 **Notices.** Legal notices must be in writing and sent to the addresses
above (attn: `[LEGAL CONTACT]`) by courier, certified mail, or email to
`[LEGAL EMAIL]` with confirmation of receipt.

15.4 **Force Majeure.** Neither Party is liable for delay or failure caused by
events beyond its reasonable control (excluding payment obligations).

15.5 **Entire Agreement; Amendment; Waiver.** This Agreement (including its
Exhibits) is the entire agreement between the Parties regarding its subject
matter and supersedes prior discussions. Amendments must be in a writing signed
by both Parties. A waiver is effective only if in writing and does not waive
future breaches.

15.6 **Severability.** If a provision is unenforceable, it will be modified to
the minimum extent necessary, and the remainder stays in effect.

15.7 **Counterparts; E-Signature.** This Agreement may be executed in
counterparts, including by electronic signature, each of which is an original.
